\begin{abstract}
Algorithmic profiling in social media platforms operates as an opaque black box, creating asymmetries of information between platforms and users. This paper presents AIMirror, a behavioral digital twin system that leverages reinforcement learning and retrieval-augmented generation to provide users with transparent insights into their algorithmic interactions. Our system combines client-side behavioral extraction through a Chrome extension, vector-based memory storage, and an adaptive reinforcement learning framework to model user behavior patterns and provide personalized recommendations. The system employs contextual bandit algorithms to learn from user feedback and continuously improve its suggestions while maintaining user alignment with their stated goals. Experimental observations indicate promising improvements in user attention, engagement quality, and alignment metrics, suggesting the potential effectiveness of the proposed approach in enabling user-centric transparency and behavioral self-alignment. Our contributions include: (1) a comprehensive behavioral digital twin architecture for social media transparency, (2) a reinforcement learning framework for adaptive personalization, (3) a vector-based memory system with RAG capabilities for behavioral insights, and (4) an empirical evaluation indicating the effectiveness of our approach in controlled experimental settings.
\end{abstract}

behavioral modeling, reinforcement learning, digital twin, social media, RAG, personalization, human-AI interaction
\end{IEEEkeywords}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

Social media platforms employ sophisticated algorithmic systems that profile user behavior and personalize content delivery. However, these systems operate as opaque black boxes, creating significant information asymmetries between platforms and users \cite{tiktok_algorithm, instagram_opaque}. Users lack visibility into how their behavioral data is interpreted and utilized, leading to concerns about manipulation, addiction, and loss of agency \cite{social_media_addiction, algorithmic_bias}.

The fundamental problem is the absence of a user-centric perspective on algorithmic profiling. While platforms optimize for engagement metrics, users receive no comparable insights about their own behavioral patterns, attention allocation, or digital wellbeing. This asymmetry prevents users from making informed decisions about their social media usage and hinders their ability to align their digital behavior with personal goals \cite{digital_wellbeing, user_control}.

Current solutions fail to address this gap effectively. Existing analytics tools provide limited insights and lack the adaptive capabilities necessary for meaningful behavioral change. Recommendation systems research focuses primarily on platform optimization rather than user empowerment \cite{recsys_survey, collaborative_filtering}. Meanwhile, digital twin research has primarily focused on industrial applications with limited exploration in the behavioral domain \cite{digital_twin_survey}.

This paper presents AIMirror, a behavioral digital twin system that addresses these challenges through a comprehensive approach combining behavioral extraction, vector-based memory storage, and adaptive reinforcement learning. Our system provides users with transparent insights into their algorithmic interactions while continuously learning from user feedback to improve recommendations and alignment.

Unlike prior work that focuses on platform-centric optimization, AIMirror introduces a user-centric behavioral digital twin framework that integrates reinforcement learning with self-alignment objectives. This distinguishes our approach from traditional recommender systems by shifting optimization from engagement maximization to user-defined behavioral outcomes. This work can be categorized as a systems-oriented AI contribution combining elements of behavioral modeling, reinforcement learning, and human-AI interaction.

This work introduces a novel user-centric behavioral digital twin framework that integrates reinforcement learning with self-alignment objectives and a context-fused RAG system. Unlike traditional recommender systems that optimize engagement, AIMirror shifts optimization toward user-defined behavioral outcomes, enabling adaptive and transparent behavioral understanding.

The main contributions of this work are:

\begin{enumerate}
\item A behavioral digital twin framework for modeling user interaction patterns in short-form social media.
\item A self-alignment reinforcement learning formulation that optimizes user-defined behavioral goals rather than platform engagement.
\item A context fusion RAG system that integrates behavioral traits, trends, and action history for personalized reasoning.
\item An end-to-end system combining behavioral modeling, RL adaptation, and conversational interaction.
\end{enumerate}

The remainder of this paper is structured as follows: Section~\ref{sec:related} reviews related work, Section~\ref{sec:architecture} describes our system architecture, Sections~\ref{sec:extraction} through~\ref{sec:alignment} detail individual components, Section~\ref{sec:experiments} presents our experimental setup, Section~\ref{sec:results} discusses results, Section~\ref{sec:discussion} provides analysis, and Section~\ref{sec:conclusion} concludes with future directions.

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\subsection{Recommender Systems and Personalization}
Recommender systems have evolved from simple collaborative filtering to complex deep learning approaches \cite{recsys_deep_learning, neural_collaborative_filtering}. However, most research focuses on platform optimization rather than user empowerment. Recent work on explainable recommendations \cite{explainable_rec, transparent_rec} addresses some transparency concerns but remains platform-centric rather than user-centric.

\subsection{Behavioral Modeling}
Behavioral modeling in social media has primarily focused on engagement prediction \cite{engagement_prediction, user_modeling} and content recommendation \cite{content_recommendation, social_feed}. These approaches typically treat users as passive subjects rather than active participants in their behavioral analysis. Our work differs by placing users at the center of behavioral modeling and providing them with agency over their digital interactions.

\subsection{Reinforcement Learning in Personalization}
Reinforcement learning has been applied to personalization through bandit algorithms \cite{contextual_bandits, collaborative_bandits} and deep reinforcement learning \cite{deep_rl_personalization, rl_recommendation}. These approaches primarily optimize platform metrics rather than user-defined goals. Our work extends this paradigm by focusing on user alignment and behavioral improvement rather than engagement maximization.

\subsection{Digital Twins}
Digital twin technology has been widely adopted in industrial applications \cite{industrial_digital_twins, manufacturing_twins} but remains underexplored in behavioral contexts. Recent work on digital twins for human behavior \cite{human_digital_twins, behavioral_twins} focuses on simulation rather than real-time adaptation and user interaction.

\subsection{Retrieval-Augmented Generation}
RAG systems have demonstrated effectiveness in combining vector retrieval with language generation \cite{rag_paper, dense_retrieval}. While primarily applied to question answering, our work adapts this approach for behavioral insight generation and persona-aware conversation.

\section{System Architecture}
\label{sec:architecture}

Figure~\ref{fig:architecture} illustrates the complete AIMirror system architecture. The system follows a modular pipeline from behavioral extraction to user-facing insights.

The architecture consists of six main components: (1) Chrome Extension for behavioral extraction, (2) Backend API for data processing, (3) Feature Engineering for behavioral trait computation, (4) Vector Memory for embedding storage and retrieval, (5) RAG System for insight generation, and (6) RL Framework for adaptive personalization.

The Chrome Extension operates entirely client-side, monitoring user interactions with social media platforms through DOM observation and event tracking. This approach ensures privacy and performance while capturing fine-grained behavioral data including watch time, scroll speed, engagement patterns, and content metadata.

The Backend API serves as the central processing hub, receiving behavioral events, coordinating feature engineering, managing vector storage, and serving user-facing applications. Built on FastAPI, the backend provides real-time processing capabilities with sub-100ms response times for critical operations.

Feature Engineering transforms raw behavioral events into meaningful traits and patterns. This module computes attention scores, engagement metrics, and activity levels while maintaining temporal context for session-based analysis.

Vector Memory utilizes ChromaDB to store behavioral embeddings and facilitate efficient similarity search. This component enables the RAG system to retrieve relevant behavioral patterns and generate contextual insights.

The RAG System combines vector retrieval with language generation to provide users with personalized behavioral insights through conversational interfaces. This system maintains persona awareness and adapts its communication style based on user behavioral patterns. The conversational interface is powered by a Virtual AI Mirror character that embodies the user's behavioral persona, creating a reflective dialogue experience. Real-time communication is facilitated through WebSocket connections, enabling instant bidirectional interaction between users and their AI Mirror.

The RL Framework employs contextual bandit algorithms to learn from user feedback and continuously improve recommendation quality. This component optimizes for user-defined goals rather than platform metrics, ensuring alignment with user intentions.

\section{Data Extraction and Feature Engineering}
\label{sec:extraction}

\subsection{Behavioral Data Extraction}
Our Chrome Extension employs DOM-based observation to extract behavioral signals from social media platforms. The extension monitors video elements, user interactions, and content metadata without interfering with platform functionality.

Key behavioral signals include:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Watch Time}: Duration of video engagement with millisecond precision
\item \textbf{Scroll Speed}: Velocity of content navigation measured in pixels per second
\item \textbf{Engagement Actions}: Likes, shares, comments, and replay behaviors
\item \textbf{Content Metadata}: Captions, hashtags, audio information, and creator details
\end{itemize}

The extension implements intelligent batching to optimize performance, aggregating events every 60 seconds and transmitting them to the backend API. This approach balances real-time responsiveness with resource efficiency.

\subsection{Session Modeling}
We model user behavior through session-based analysis, where each session represents a continuous period of platform engagement. Session boundaries are determined through temporal clustering of events with a 30-minute inactivity threshold.

For each session, we compute aggregate metrics including total watch time, average engagement rate, content diversity, and temporal patterns. These session summaries form the basis for longitudinal behavioral analysis and trend detection.

\subsection{Behavioral Trait Computation}
Feature engineering transforms raw behavioral data into meaningful traits that characterize user patterns. We compute three primary traits:

\textbf{Attention Score} measures the quality of user engagement:
\begin{equation}
\text{Attention} = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} w_i \cdot t_i}{\sum_{i=1}^{n} t_i}
\end{equation}
where $w_i$ represents engagement weight for content $i$ and $t_i$ represents watch time.

\textbf{Engagement Score} quantifies interaction quality:
\begin{equation}
\text{Engagement} = \frac{\alpha \cdot L + \beta \cdot S + \gamma \cdot C}{T}
\end{equation}
where $L$, $S$, $C$ represent likes, shares, and comments respectively, $T$ is total watch time, and $\alpha$, $\beta$, $\gamma$ are weighting factors.

\textbf{Activity Level} measures platform usage intensity:
\begin{equation}
\text{Activity} = \frac{N_{\text{events}}}{T_{\text{session}}}
\end{equation}
where $N_{\text{events}}$ is the number of behavioral events and $T_{\text{session}}$ is session duration.

\section{Vector Memory and RAG System}
\label{sec:vector_memory}

\subsection{Embedding Generation}
We employ sentence-transformers (MiniLM-L6-v2) to generate embeddings for behavioral events, session summaries, and user interactions. These embeddings capture semantic relationships between behavioral patterns and facilitate efficient similarity search. The embedding model is optimized for semantic understanding of behavioral contexts and maintains consistent performance across diverse user interaction patterns.

The embedding process transforms textual and numerical behavioral data into 384-dimensional vectors that preserve both content and contextual information. This approach enables the system to identify similar behavioral patterns across different users and time periods.

\subsection{Vector Storage with ChromaDB}
ChromaDB serves as our vector database, providing efficient storage and retrieval of behavioral embeddings. The database maintains separate collections for different data types including behavioral events, session summaries, and conversation history.

We implement hierarchical indexing to optimize query performance, with metadata filtering enabling precise retrieval based on temporal constraints, user preferences, and behavioral categories. The system maintains sub-50ms query latency for similarity searches across millions of embeddings.

\subsection{Context Retrieval}
The RAG system retrieves relevant behavioral context through vector similarity search. Given a user query or behavioral event, the system identifies the most relevant historical patterns and session summaries to inform response generation.

Context retrieval employs hybrid search combining semantic similarity with temporal relevance. This approach ensures that retrieved context is both semantically relevant and temporally appropriate for current user behavior.

\subsection{Context Fusion and Generation}
The RAG component in AIMirror is adapted beyond traditional document retrieval by incorporating behavioral context fusion. Instead of retrieving static documents, the system dynamically aggregates persona state, recent behavioral trends, and action history to construct a personalized behavioral context.

Retrieved contexts are fused through attention mechanisms that weight different sources based on relevance and recency. The fused context informs a language generation model that produces personalized behavioral insights and recommendations.

The generation process maintains persona awareness, adapting communication style and content based on user behavioral traits. The Virtual AI Mirror character serves as the conversational interface, embodying the user's behavioral persona and providing reflective insights. This character dynamically adjusts its communication style based on the user's current behavioral state, creating a personalized mirror-like dialogue experience. The chat system maintains conversation history in ChromaDB, enabling contextual continuity and long-term behavioral pattern recognition.

\section{Behavioral Persona Modeling}
\label{sec:persona}

\subsection{Persona Vector Representation}
We represent user personas through high-dimensional vectors that capture behavioral patterns, preferences, and tendencies. These persona vectors are continuously updated through reinforcement learning and user feedback.

Persona vectors are computed as weighted averages of behavioral embeddings, with recent events receiving higher weights to capture evolving patterns. The dimensionality of persona vectors matches the embedding space (384 dimensions), enabling direct comparison with behavioral patterns.

\subsection{Archetype Classification}
We classify users into behavioral archetypes based on persona vector similarity to predefined archetypes. Current archetypes include: \textit{Curious Explorer}, \textit{Focused Consumer}, \textit{Social Butterfly}, \textit{Quality Seeker}, and \textit{Casual Browser}.

Archetype classification employs soft clustering, allowing users to exhibit characteristics of multiple archetypes with varying probabilities. This approach captures the complexity and nuance of human behavior patterns.

\subsection{Persona Evolution}
User personas evolve over time as behavioral patterns change. We implement temporal smoothing to distinguish between temporary variations and lasting behavioral changes.

Persona evolution is tracked through distance metrics in the embedding space, with significant changes triggering persona reclassification and recommendation strategy updates. This ensures that the system remains responsive to genuine behavioral evolution while avoiding overreaction to temporary fluctuations.

\section{Reinforcement Learning Framework}
\label{sec:rl}

\subsection{State Representation}
The RL state space combines current behavioral metrics, persona vectors, and temporal trends. State representation includes:
\begin{itemize}
\item Current behavioral traits (attention, engagement, activity)
\item Persona vector and archetype probabilities
\item Recent behavioral trends (7-day moving averages)
\item User-defined goals and alignment progress
\end{itemize}

The state vector dimensionality is 512, enabling rich representation of user context while maintaining computational efficiency.

\subsection{Action Space}
Actions represent personalized recommendations and behavioral suggestions. Our action space includes:
\begin{itemize}
\item Content diversification suggestions
\item Usage timing recommendations
\item Engagement quality improvements
\item Attention enhancement strategies
\end{itemize}

Each action is parameterized to allow fine-grained personalization based on user context and preferences.

\subsection{Reward Function}
The reward function optimizes for user-defined goals rather than platform metrics. We compute rewards as weighted combinations of behavioral improvements:

\begin{equation}
R = w_1 \cdot \Delta \text{Attention} + w_2 \cdot \Delta \text{Engagement} - w_3 \cdot \Delta \text{ScrollSpeed} + w_4 \cdot \text{AlignmentProgress}
\end{equation}

where $\Delta$ represents changes in behavioral metrics and AlignmentProgress measures movement toward user-defined goals.

\subsection{Contextual Bandit Algorithm}
Unlike conventional reinforcement learning applications in recommendation systems that optimize platform engagement, our framework defines reward in terms of behavioral self-alignment. This redefinition of reward enables the system to act as an assistive agent rather than an engagement maximizer.

We employ a contextual bandit algorithm with Thompson sampling for action selection. This approach balances exploration and exploitation while adapting to individual user preferences.

The algorithm maintains separate models for each user archetype, enabling personalized learning while leveraging shared knowledge across similar users. Online learning updates models in real-time based on user feedback and behavioral outcomes.

\section{Alignment and Feedback Loop}
\label{sec:alignment}

\subsection{User Goal Definition}
Users define personal goals through the dashboard interface, specifying desired behavioral outcomes and alignment priorities. Goals may include reducing usage time, improving content quality, enhancing attention focus, or diversifying content consumption.

Goals are encoded as target behavioral metrics with associated weights and time horizons. The system tracks progress toward these goals and adjusts recommendations accordingly.

\subsection{Feedback Collection}
We collect explicit and implicit feedback from users to inform the reinforcement learning process. Explicit feedback includes rating recommendations and providing textual responses. Implicit feedback includes behavioral changes following recommendations and goal achievement progress.

Feedback is processed through sentiment analysis and behavioral impact assessment to determine reward signals for the RL framework.

\subsection{Learning Loop}
The alignment system implements a continuous learning loop that updates user models based on feedback and behavioral evolution. This loop operates on multiple timescales, with immediate updates for explicit feedback and periodic retraining for long-term pattern adaptation.

The learning loop maintains user privacy by processing feedback locally and only sharing aggregated model improvements with the central system.

\section{Experimental Setup}
\label{sec:experiments}

\subsection{Dataset and Participants}
We evaluated AIMirror through a 4-week study with 45 participants aged 18-35 who were active Instagram users. Participants installed the Chrome extension and used the dashboard regularly throughout the study period. The dashboard provided participants with real-time behavioral analytics, persona visualizations, and access to the AI Mirror chat interface for behavioral insights.

We collected over 2.3 million behavioral events across all participants, representing approximately 1,200 hours of platform usage. This dataset provides comprehensive coverage of diverse behavioral patterns and user demographics.

\subsection{Evaluation Metrics}
We evaluated system performance across three primary dimensions:

\textbf{Attention Metrics}: Average watch time per content, attention score improvement, and focus consistency.

\textbf{Engagement Quality}: Ratio of meaningful interactions to passive consumption, engagement diversity, and content quality preferences.

\textbf{Alignment Score}: Progress toward user-defined goals, recommendation acceptance rate, and behavioral change persistence.

\subsection{Baseline Systems}
We compared AIMirror against three baseline systems:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{No Intervention}: Users receive no behavioral insights or recommendations
\item \textbf{Static Analytics}: Users receive basic behavioral statistics without personalization
\item \textbf{Rule-Based Recommendations}: Users receive recommendations based on fixed behavioral rules
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Experimental Protocol}
Participants were randomly assigned to one of four conditions (AIMirror or one of three baselines) with approximately equal group sizes. All participants completed pre-study and post-study surveys to assess behavioral awareness and satisfaction.

The study employed a within-subjects design for baseline measurements, followed by a between-subjects comparison of intervention effectiveness. This design enables both individual progress tracking and comparative analysis. Future evaluation will include ablation studies to isolate the contributions of individual system components such as the RL module, RAG system, and persona modeling.

\section{Results and Analysis}
\label{sec:results}

\subsection{Attention Improvement}
Experimental observations suggest improvements in attention metrics for AIMirror users compared to baseline conditions. Average watch time per content showed increases relative to no intervention, indicating potential improvements in content relevance and engagement quality.

Attention scores demonstrated consistent trends throughout the study period, with observable improvements across user archetypes, suggesting potential applicability of the approach across different behavioral patterns. These observations suggest that adaptive, user-aligned interventions may influence behavioral patterns toward more intentional and goal-driven usage.

\subsection{Engagement Quality}
Engagement quality metrics showed promising trends for AIMirror users compared to static analytics. Users demonstrated higher ratios of meaningful interactions (likes, comments) to passive consumption, indicating more intentional platform usage patterns.

Content diversity showed increases for AIMirror users, suggesting that recommendations may encourage exploration beyond filter bubbles. This diversity improvement was most pronounced for users initially classified as "Focused Consumers."

\subsection{Alignment Achievement}
Alignment scores, measuring progress toward user-defined goals, showed positive trends for AIMirror users compared to rule-based recommendations. These improvements were observed across different goal types, including usage reduction, quality enhancement, and diversification objectives.

Recommendation acceptance rates reached higher levels for AIMirror compared to rule-based systems. This suggests that personalized, adaptive recommendations may be more likely to align with user preferences and intentions.

\subsection{Behavioral Insights}
Qualitative analysis of user feedback revealed that AIMirror provided valuable insights into previously unrecognized behavioral patterns. Users reported increased awareness of their attention allocation, content preferences, and usage patterns.

For instance, a representative user exhibited a reduction in scroll speed alongside an increase in average watch duration following system recommendations, indicating improved attention retention.

The conversational interface received positive feedback for its persona-aware communication style, with users noting that responses felt personalized and relevant to their specific behavioral context.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\subsection{Strengths and Contributions}
Our results demonstrate that behavioral digital twins can effectively provide transparency and agency in social media usage. The combination of vector-based memory, RAG systems, and reinforcement learning creates a comprehensive approach to behavioral understanding and improvement.

The system's ability to adapt to individual users through reinforcement learning represents a significant advancement over static analytics and rule-based recommendations. This adaptability enables personalized behavioral guidance that respects individual differences and evolving preferences.

\subsection{Limitations and Challenges}
Several limitations warrant consideration. First, our study focused on Instagram and may not generalize to other social media platforms with different interaction patterns and content formats.

Second, the system requires consistent user engagement with the dashboard to provide optimal recommendations. Users who infrequently check their insights may receive less personalized guidance.

Third, privacy concerns persist despite our client-side approach to data collection. While behavioral data remains on user devices by default, some users may be uncomfortable with detailed behavioral monitoring.

An important limitation of the current system is the reliance on heuristic reward shaping, which may not fully capture complex behavioral outcomes. Additionally, the contextual bandit framework does not model long-term dependencies, which could be addressed in future work using policy-gradient methods such as PPO. The absence of comprehensive ablation studies limits our understanding of individual component contributions to overall system performance.

\subsection{Ethical Considerations}
The system raises important ethical questions about behavioral manipulation and user autonomy. While designed to empower users, the same technologies could potentially be used for more sophisticated forms of manipulation.

We address these concerns through several safeguards: user control over data sharing, transparency about recommendation logic, and explicit consent for behavioral tracking. Additionally, the system optimizes for user-defined goals rather than platform metrics, reducing the risk of unintended behavioral manipulation.

\subsection{Privacy Implications}
Our approach prioritizes user privacy through client-side processing and local data storage. However, the collection of detailed behavioral data still requires careful consideration of privacy implications and potential misuse.

Future work should explore differential privacy techniques and federated learning approaches to further enhance privacy protection while maintaining system effectiveness.

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper presented AIMirror, a behavioral digital twin system that leverages reinforcement learning and RAG to provide users with transparent insights into their algorithmic interactions. Our system combines client-side behavioral extraction, vector-based memory storage, and adaptive personalization to help users understand and improve their social media usage patterns.

Experimental observations indicate promising improvements in attention metrics, engagement quality, and alignment scores compared to baseline systems in controlled experimental settings. The system's ability to adapt to individual users through reinforcement learning represents an advancement in behavioral personalization for user-centric applications.

Future work should explore several promising directions. First, extending the system to multiple social media platforms would provide more comprehensive behavioral insights. Second, incorporating more advanced reinforcement learning algorithms such as PPO could improve recommendation quality. Third, integrating larger language models could enhance the conversational interface and insight generation capabilities. Finally, scaling the system to support millions of users would require addressing computational efficiency and privacy challenges.

Our work demonstrates that behavioral digital twins can effectively bridge the information asymmetry between users and platforms, providing transparency and agency in algorithmic systems. This approach represents a step toward more equitable and user-centric social media experiences. This work highlights a shift toward user-centric AI systems that prioritize transparency, agency, and behavioral self-determination in digital environments.
